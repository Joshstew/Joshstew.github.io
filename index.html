As a Cloud Technology Consultant specializing in the Platform as a Service (PaaS) practice, I often find myself wearing different hats. This has been a blessing, as my projects require me to constantly adapt and learn new technologies, which feeds my natural intellectual curiousity.

As a First-Generation American & First-Generation College graduate, I was fortunate to have the help and support of friends, family, and mentors. Having experienced the transformative power of a strong support network, I've committed to serving the community and the development of others.

Service

A principle I picked up from my late father -- always leave a place in better shape than how you found it. I've taken that principle and committed myself to serving the communities I belong to and to the personal & professional development of others.

While at the University of Michigan, I was a Founding Member of a professional business fraternity, Phi Beta Lambda. The organization has grown from the original 20 Founding Members and expanded to an organization of over 80 young professionals, guided by the principles of Service, Education, and Progress.

As I've settled in the Boston area I've committed to giving back to my community by volunteering at Minds Matter Boston. During my time in the organization I've had the priviledge of serving as a co-mentor to an exeptional bright and determined young woman, as she prepares herself for the college search and application process. Additionally, I serve on the Volunteer Leadership Team as a Member @ Large, assisting the organizations efforts to streamline their analytics & reporting.

If you are interested in volunteering at Minds Matter Boston, please feel free to contact me directly for more information or click here. Additionally, I provide pro-bono technical & strategy consulting services for non-profits.
